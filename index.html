Hi, I'm Lorena — a recent Electronics Engineering graduate with a growing passion for software development. My background bridges both electronics and programming, giving me a flexible foundation for IT and development roles.

I started exploring web design at 13 by customizing HTML/CSS templates on phpBB forums. Later in high school, even though I was in the Natural Sciences specialization, the small amount of programming we did was enough to make me choose Informatics as my third subject for the Baccalaureate exam, knowing I wanted to pursue that field further.

Most of my knowledge comes from real, hands-on projects, which has shaped my approach, I thrive when I can directly apply what I'm learning. I find that solving problems and addressing needs as they arise helps me retain knowledge and turn complex ideas into practical solutions.

While I'm exploring the wide range of roles in tech, I'm especially interested in applying my skills in software development—whether that's web, mobile, or backend. I see myself as a flexible developer, ready to take on challenges, learn quickly, and grow into a specialized role through real-world experience.

Hobbies
I've loved writing since I was 11 — from original stories to collaborative role-plays (similar to DnD) on online forums. It helped me develop creativity and also introduced me to web design through customizing forum layouts and character sheets. I enjoy world-building in medieval fantasy and post-apocalyptic settings.
My passion for reading surfaced when I was 10, finding fantasy and supernatural stories in both medieval and modern settings especially entertaining. My favorite series is The Wardstone Chronicles by Joseph Delaney, and I also enjoy crime fiction, particularly Camilla Läckberg's work. Reading keeps me inspired and fuels the world-building ideas behind many of my own creative projects.
I've been passionate about gaming since childhood, with a strong preference for single-player FPS and RPG titles. The Assassin's Creed series is a personal favorite, though I also enjoy multiplayer games like MMORPGs, League of Legends, and CS2. Character creation is one of my favorite parts of gaming, and it naturally ties into my passion for building new stories and worlds.
Crochet is a hobby that I picked up only a couple of years ago, inspired by one of my friends. I don't practice it very often, as I prefer having a specific need or purpose for any project I start and I tend to think it overflow a lot, but I really enjoy it and I find crocheting very relaxing and therapeutic.
Music has always been a constant in my life. I listen to it most of the time (even in the background) and it always fuels me with energy. Over the years, I've grown more interested in it than I ever expected. I enjoyed learning about music history, played a wooden bass xylophone in school, and I'm currently playing both the soprano and alto recorder. Even without formal training, exploring music has become a meaningful and steady interest for me.
